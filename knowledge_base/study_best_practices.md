# Acme Corp Study Best Practices & Readiness Criteria

**Document ID:** LRN-BP-005
**Owner:** Acme Corp Learning & Enablement Office
**Status:** Synthetic reference content for demonstration only.

## Recommended study pattern

- Study in **focused blocks of 1–2 hours**; sessions longer than 2 hours show diminishing retention.
- Schedule a **weekly review checkpoint** to consolidate the week's milestones and re-test weak skills.
- **Space practice** across the week rather than cramming; aim for at least 4 study days per week.
- Alternate **theory and hands-on labs**; practical skills (pipelines, data processing, architecture) need applied practice, not reading alone.

## Sequencing guidance

- Cover **foundational skills before advanced skills**. For example, complete Identity & Access Basics before Security Architecture, and CI/CD Pipelines before Secure DevOps.
- Front-load skills with the **largest exam weighting** and the learner's **largest skill gaps**.

## Exam-readiness criteria

A learner is considered **ready to sit the exam** only when **both** conditions are met:
1. They have completed **at least the recommended study hours** for the certification (see LRN-CAT-001), and
2. Their **average practice score is at or above the target** for that certification (75% for Associate/Professional; 80% for Expert).

Readiness bands used in reporting:
- **On track** — on pace to meet both conditions within the timeline.
- **At risk** — likely to meet only one condition by the deadline; plan adjustment recommended.
- **Not ready** — currently below recommended hours and below target practice score.

## Observed outcome pattern

Historical (synthetic) program data shows learners who complete **more than 20 study hours** and reach **more than 75% on practice scores** have markedly stronger certification outcomes. Falling short on either dimension is the leading predictor of a failed first attempt.
