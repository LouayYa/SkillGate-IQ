## Purpose

Describes how work capacity affects learning completion, so study plans can be made realistic against a learner's meeting load and focus time. Used for capacity-aware scheduling and manager-level risk insights.

## Key correlations (synthetic)

- Employees with **more than 20 meeting hours per week** show **lower study completion** rates and are frequently flagged "At risk."
- Optimal completion is observed when a learner has **12–18 meeting hours per week** and **at least 15 focus hours per week**.
- Learners with **fewer than 8 focus hours per week** rarely complete recommended study hours within a standard timeline, regardless of intent.

## Scheduling guidance

- Place study blocks in the learner's **preferred learning slot** and within **focus-heavy periods**, not adjacent to back-to-back meeting days.
- If a learner's available focus hours over the timeline are **less than the certification's recommended study hours**, the plan is over-capacity — either extend the timeline or reduce weekly scope, and flag the constraint.
